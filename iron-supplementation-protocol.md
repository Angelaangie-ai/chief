# Iron Supplementation Protocol for Ferritin Recovery (Ferritin: 4 ng/mL)

## Severity Assessment
Ferritin of 4 ng/mL = **severe iron depletion** (normal: 30-150 ng/mL for women; optimal for longevity: 50-100 ng/mL). At this level, IV iron should be discussed with a physician as first-line, with oral supplementation as maintenance/alternative.

## Evidence-Based Oral Protocol

### Dosing — Alternate-Day, Single Morning Dose
**Key finding (Stoffel et al., 2020; von Siebenthal et al., 2023):**
- Oral iron doses ≥60 mg trigger a hepcidin spike lasting ~24h that blocks further absorption
- Alternate-day dosing maximizes fractional absorption
- Fewer GI side effects (56% reduction) with equal or better ferritin outcomes at 6 months

### Recommended Regimen
- **Form**: Ferrous bisglycinate (gentler) or ferrous sulfate (cheaper, well-studied)
- **Dose**: 100-120 mg elemental iron per dose
- **Frequency**: Every other day (Mon/Wed/Fri/Sun rotation)
- **Timing**: Morning, on empty stomach (1h before or 2h after food)
- **Duration**: 3-6 months minimum; continue until ferritin >50 ng/mL

### Cofactors (Take WITH iron)
- **Vitamin C**: 200-500 mg — enhances non-heme iron absorption 2-3x
- **Avoid within 2h**: calcium, coffee, tea, dairy, antacids, zinc

### Cofactors (Take on OFF days or separate by 4h+)
- **B12 & folate** — often co-deficient; support erythropoiesis
- **Copper** — needed for ceruloplasmin/iron metabolism (1-2 mg/day)
- **Vitamin A** — supports iron mobilization from stores

## Monitoring Protocol
- **Baseline**: Ferritin, serum iron, TIBC, transferrin sat, CBC, reticulocyte count, CRP
- **4-6 weeks**: Ferritin, CBC, reticulocyte count (Hb rising, reticulocytes up)
- **3 months**: Full iron panel + CBC (target: ferritin >30 ng/mL)
- **6 months**: Full iron panel + CBC (target: ferritin >50 ng/mL)
- **Ongoing**: Every 3-6 months until stable (maintain 50-100 ng/mL)

**Always check CRP** — ferritin is an acute phase reactant; inflammation falsely elevates it.

## IV Iron Consideration
At ferritin = 4, strongly recommend discussing **IV iron infusion** (ferric carboxymaltose / Injectafer):
- Single 750-1000 mg infusion can raise ferritin to normal range in 1-2 weeks
- Oral iron alone may take 4-6 months to reach target

## Expected Timeline (Oral Only)
- Weeks 1-2: Reticulocyte count rises
- Weeks 4-8: Hemoglobin normalizes (if anemic)
- Months 3-6: Ferritin reaches 30-50 ng/mL
- Months 6-12: Ferritin reaches optimal 50-100 ng/mL

## Root Cause Investigation
Must identify WHY ferritin is 4: heavy menstrual bleeding, GI malabsorption, dietary insufficiency, or chronic blood loss.
